About Burt Herman

I build the future of trusted media at the intersection of journalism, technology and community. By connecting diverse communities and developing new tools, I help newsrooms innovate and grow while maintaining their commitment to public trust.

As co-founder and principal of Hacks/Hackers, I've helped create one of the world's most influential movements bridging journalism and technology. We're currently leading critical conversations around AI's transformative potential for media, while ensuring these powerful technologies serve democracy and foster trust.

I co-founded Storify, pioneering social media curation and helping establish new standards for real-time storytelling that reached millions. The platform was acquired by Livefyre and later Adobe. I also helped launch the Lenfest Institute for Journalism, where we developed innovative approaches to sustaining local news, and most recently served as CEO of Lede, building modern publishing tools for independent newsrooms.

My career began as a foreign correspondent for The Associated Press, reporting from Korea, Moscow, Central Asia and Berlin for over a decade. I'm a JSK fellow at Stanford University, where I also earned bachelor's and master's degrees and served as editor in chief of The Stanford Daily.
