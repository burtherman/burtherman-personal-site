About Burt Herman

As co-founder and principal of Hacks/Hackers, I've spent more than 15 years connecting journalists with technologists. The network grew from a San Francisco meetup in 2010 to dozens of chapters worldwide. Today the organization is focused on AI — convening practitioners around journalism's AI moment and helping newsrooms put new tools to work.

I also co-founded Storify in 2010 — a platform for curating social media into coherent narratives at a moment when that felt new. It reached millions of users before being acquired by Livefyre, then Adobe. I helped launch the Lenfest Institute for Journalism, working on how to sustain local news. I later served as a product director on the Condé Nast technology platform team, and most recently as CEO of Lede, building publishing tools for independent newsrooms.

Before all that, I reported for The Associated Press from Korea, Moscow, Central Asia and Berlin for more than a decade. I was a JSK fellow at Stanford, where I earned my bachelor's and master's degrees and served as editor-in-chief of The Stanford Daily.
